# Go-To-Market (GTM) Strategy

This document outlines the startup-centric distribution strategy for the SpendLens MVP, focusing on organic acquisition and high-leverage community channels.

## Target User Profile

**Primary Persona:** Seed-stage startup founders with 3–20 developers who are rapidly adopting AI coding tools without centralized procurement oversight. 

**The Pain Point:** In fast-moving engineering teams, individual contributors often expense disparate AI tools (Cursor, Copilot, ChatGPT, Claude) on fragmented corporate cards. This leads to massive tier overlaps (e.g., paying for both ChatGPT Team and Claude Pro) and wasted seats (e.g., paying for Enterprise plans when team sizes don't meet the threshold). The founder wants visibility and cost consolidation without slowing down developer velocity.

## Acquisition Channels

To maximize initial visibility and leverage the platform's "viral share" functionality, we will focus on zero-cost, high-leverage distribution channels where our target persona actively seeks operational and growth advice.

1. **HackerNews (Show HN):** A direct, technically-focused launch. The audience appreciates transparent, math-based utility tools. We will position SpendLens as a "weekend project" that exposes how much SaaS companies are overcharging startups.
2. **Twitter Founder Circles:** Leveraging "Build in Public" dynamics. We will share anonymized, high-shock-value screenshots (e.g., "This startup was wasting $4,800/yr on unused Cursor Business seats") and link directly to the SpendLens public share URLs.
3. **r/startups:** Engaging in discussions regarding burn rate optimization, runway extension, and SaaS bloat. SpendLens serves as a free, instantly valuable diagnostic tool for these threads.
4. **IndieHackers:** Positioning the tool as an operational hack for bootstrapped founders looking to extend their runway by aggressively trimming infrastructure and subscription fat.

## The Viral Loop

The product itself acts as a distribution engine. When a founder generates an audit, they are prompted with a highly polished, OG-tag optimized public share URL. By sharing this link internally with their team or externally with their founder network (e.g., "Look how much we just recovered"), they inadvertently market SpendLens to other decision-makers.
